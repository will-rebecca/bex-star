Sociology Research

The Relation Between Socioeconomic Status & Trauma

Trauma is a deeply distressing and or disruptive experience that can have lasting effects that negatively impact a person’s well-being. While many students encounter difficulties during the course of their academic adventure, those from lower-income households may have been disproportionately exposed to traumatic experiences both before and also while they attended college.

Methodologies

Research Design
  • Hypothesis: We suggested that college students' trauma exposure related to their family income level.
  • Survey-based Quantitative design
  • Cross-sectional design
  • Aimed to measure different traumas students might have undergone.
Sample
  • The target population for this study included college students aged 18–25.
  • Students were grouped based on the highest level of education attained by their parents; parents with less than high school education and no higher education
Data Collection Procedure
  • Online QR code from Google Forms survey distributed through university mailing lists and word of mouth.
  • Participation was voluntary, and responses remained anonymous to ensure confidentiality.
  • Participants were informed about the study’s purpose and prompted with a disclaimer for the study’s demographic requirements before completing the survey.
  • The ethical considerations included ensuring participants’ privacy and warning participants of sensitive information.
Measures & Survey
  • To assess trauma exposure, we used the Adverse Childhood Experience (ACE) test
  • To capture experiences specific to LGBTQ+ individuals, we added adapted items from LGBTQ+ ACE tools
  • Additional questions were incorporated to measure the experience of racial discrimination or trauma
  • parental education will be collected through self-reported data on the level of education attained by the participant's parents. This served as a proxy for household income.

Research Findings

The demographic data presents a valuable overview of student backgrounds, many of which show indicators of economic or educational disadvantage.

Participant Demographic
  • A total of 38 college students participated
  • 75% (27) stated they were attending college full time
  • 54.1% (20 participants) identified with she/her pronouns
  • 75.7% (28) reported living with their parents
  • 48.6 (18) identified as first-generation college students
Data trends

Students from lower socioeconomic backgrounds, particularly those whose parents had not completed high school, reported higher average trauma scores.

It’s important to note that there were relatively few participants who reported parental education levels at the Associate, Doctorate, Some High School, or Elementary/Primary levels. This limited representation can introduce skewness into the data, as averages based on very small sample sizes may not accurately reflect broader patterns.
